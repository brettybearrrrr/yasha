Core Treatment Principles

Client-Centered Care

Every therapeutic relationship is built on the foundation of unconditional positive regard, empathic understanding, and genuine therapeutic presence. Clients are viewed as the experts of their own experience, with therapy serving as a collaborative process of exploration and growth.

Cultural Competence

Recognition and integration of cultural, ethnic, socioeconomic, and identity factors that influence individual experience. Treatment approaches are adapted to honor diverse backgrounds and lived experiences.

Trauma-Informed Framework

All clinical work operates from a trauma-informed perspective, recognizing the widespread impact of trauma and prioritizing safety, trustworthiness, and empowerment in the therapeutic process.

Systemic Perspective

Understanding individuals within the context of their relationships, family systems, and broader social environments. Treatment addresses both individual healing and systemic patterns that may contribute to presenting concerns.
